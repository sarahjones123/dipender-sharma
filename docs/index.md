# Welcome 👋

I’m **Dipender Sharma (he/him)**, a technical writer with more than a decade of experience creating documentation for SaaS products, APIs, and developer tooling. Over the years, I’ve worked at the intersection of **technology and communication**, helping organizations transform complex concepts into content that feels clear, approachable, and useful.

At **nGenue**, I took on the challenge of scaling our product documentation into a **25K+ user knowledgebase**. This included:

- Producing API documentation for distributed pipelines.
- Authoring **200+ new help articles** to support users across different workflows.
- Building a **structured docs system** that grows with the product.

My approach blends **docs-as-code workflows** (Hugo, Docusaurus, MkDocs) with modern development practices. I work with **GitHub Actions, automation pipelines, and programmatic diagrams** to ensure documentation is accurate, scalable, and always up to date.

Most recently, I built the **nGenue Knowledgebase site using MkDocs Material**. I chose this approach because I wanted something lightweight, developer-friendly, and easy to maintain—yet professional enough to serve thousands of users. The site now acts as a **single source of truth** where end users, developers, and stakeholders can access product information with ease.

---

## A little about me ✨

Beyond tools and workflows, what excites me most about documentation is the **impact it has on people**. I believe good documentation is not just about clarity—it’s about **building confidence**. When someone reads a guide I’ve written and says, *“I can do this now,”* that’s the real win for me.

I enjoy blending my **developer mindset** with a **user-first writing approach**, so I can bridge the gap between technical depth and everyday usability. Over the years, I’ve learned that great documentation doesn’t just explain a product—it shapes how people experience it.

Outside of work, I’m always curious about new ways to make knowledge more accessible—whether through automation, better information design, or simplifying how we tell complex stories.
