# Building the nGenue Knowledgebase

When I was asked to create a documentation site for **nGenue**, my first thought was: *how do I make it both simple to manage and pleasant to use?*

I explored a few options—Confluence, WordPress, and other content management systems—but most of them felt heavy, costly, or too complex for what we needed. What I really wanted was something lightweight, developer-friendly, and scalable.

That’s when I discovered **MkDocs Material**.

It checked all the right boxes:

- Works with **Markdown/AsciiDoc**, my everyday writing format.
- Provides a **modern, clean, and intuitive UI** for readers.
- Offers a **powerful built-in search** to quickly find information.
- Is **open-source**, so no licensing costs.
- Fits perfectly with **GitHub/Bitbucket version control**, making updates smooth and collaborative.

With **MkDocs Material**, I built the **nGenue Knowledgebase site,** a single, structured space where users, developers, and stakeholders can access up-to-date product documentation anytime.

This wasn’t just about choosing a tool; it was about creating a **sustainable documentation system,** something that is easy to maintain, yet professional enough to reflect the quality of our product.
